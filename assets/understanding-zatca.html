Understanding ZATCA's Phase 2 E-Invoicing Mandates

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia has taken steps forwarded to modernize its tax systems, the most notable of which being the implementation of the Zakat, Tax, and Customs Authority (ZATCA) E-Invoicing Mandates. These rules are intended to smoothening the invoicing process and increase transparency, making it easier for businesses and governments to manage taxes. The first phase of the e-invoicing system was successfully implemented, and firms should now prepare for Phase 2.

What is ZATCA's Phase 2 E-Invoicing Mandate?

PHASE 2 was implemented as of January 1st, 2023. In this phase it was requested that the taxpayer’s system would be integrated with ZATCA and generate an output file containing all e-invoices and their respective CDNs. This file is then transmitted to ZATCA.

ZATCA issued the Phase notification letters to taxpayers together with the criteria and earliest implementation date being January 1st, 2023.

On July 26th, ZATCA released Wave 14 announcing that businesses that are registered under KSA VAT and had turnover more than SAR5m for either year (2022 or exemption period ending in March 2023) must integrate their systems with Fatoorah Portal by February1st ,2025.

e-Invoicing Phase 2 Regulations

ZATCA had issued phase 2 regulations on 28th May 2021, which included phase 1 and phase 2 requirements. However, it was stated by ZATCA that the requirements provided under phase 2 of e-invoicing will be reviewed and updated in due course. Subsequently, ZATCA has now amended & released the final phase 2 regulations on 24th June 2022.

Who is Affected?

Businesses that reach the specified revenue entrance are required to comply with Phase 2 rules. ZATCA has taken a phased approach, gradually extending the deadline for compliance based on firm size and income.

Phase 2 is being rolled out in waves, with each wave targeting a certain category of taxpayers. As a result, it is important for enterprises to stay updated about their allotted schedule and ensure that their systems are prepared for integration with ZATCA.
